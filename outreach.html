Undergraduate Teaching Assistant (UGTA)

As a UGTA for the KU Physics & Astronomy Department, I support courses like Stellar Astronomy. My responsibilities include grading assignments and providing one-on-one help to students, clarifying complex topics and fostering a deeper understanding of the material. My success in this role has led to requests from multiple astronomy professors to TA for their courses in the upcoming year.

Society of Physics Students (SPS)

As an active member of the KU chapter of the Society of Physics Students, I assist with leadership tasks and have co-led the logistics and planning for conference attendance. I also work with the group to build a strong community for physics and Astronomy majors by hosting social events, organizing faculty panels, and conducting outreach for local schools.

Public Observing Night Volunteer

I frequently volunteer at the KU Physics & Astronomy Department's public observing nights. At these events, I operate telescopes for the community, helping them observe objects like the Moon and planets. I also engage with the community in discussions over what they saw in the Planetarium shows, especially when it relates to Black Holes or Cosmology, answering questions and sharing the excitement of astronomy.

In-Class Presentation: DESI DR2

In March 2025, I gave a presentation to my peers on the latest findings from the Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument (DESI) Data Release 2. I summarized the key cosmological results, explained the survey's methodology, and fielded questions on its implications for our understanding of dark energy.
