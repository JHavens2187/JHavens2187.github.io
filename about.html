The Scientist

I view the universe as a dataset—messy, chaotic, and governed by impersonal laws—but one that we can understand through rigorous inquiry.

My work focuses on Active Galactic Nuclei (AGN) and the early universe. Using data from the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST), I analyze the spectral fingerprints of cosmic dust to reveal the hidden physics of supermassive black holes. For me, science isn't just a career; it's the fulfillment of a lifelong fight for the right to ask questions.

The Systems Architect

I love building logic engines that create order out of chaos. Whether it's managing real-time tournament brackets or predicting NFL outcomes, I focus on the architecture of the solution.

Recently, I developed a machine learning pipeline that ingests 20 years of NFL statistics, removes external biases, and utilizes ensemble modeling to identify edge cases against Vegas odds. I enjoy the high-level design of software—the flow of logic and state—just as much as the code itself.

Values & Worldview

Unapologetic Leftist

I stand firmly on the political Left. My worldview is defined by a commitment to Class Solidarity, Anti-Fascism, and Human Rights. I believe that the greatest divide in our nation is the class divide, and that true scientific progress is impossible without economic justice ("Eat the Rich" isn't just a slogan; it's a call for equity).

Science is Political

I once believed science could be apolitical, but as intellectualism comes under fire, neutrality is no longer an option. Science belongs to everyone, which means we must fight for a society where everyone—regardless of class, identity, or background—has the material security to succeed.

Identity & Rights

As a bisexual man in STEM, I advocate for a field that is not just diverse in numbers but radical in its inclusion. I stand in full solidarity with the LGBTQ+ community and the liberation of oppressed peoples globally—from the fight for Trans rights to the movement for a Free Palestine.

The Origin Story

"My passion for science was not given to me; it was forged. I grew up in an environment where the output of millennia of human research was often dismissed because it didn't match a specific narrative. Navigating that conflict gave me a profound appreciation for the scientific process—not just as a body of facts, but as a method of finding truth."

The Spark
A Brief History of Time

Reading Hawking in 8th grade ignited a curiosity that couldn't be satisfied by the answers I was being given. It was the first time I realized the universe was larger, older, and more complex than I had been told.

The Conflict
Faith vs. Fact

My high school years were a daily battle. When a church elder warned me that accepting the Big Bang would "send me to hell," it didn't break my resolve; it solidified it. Later, in a "Science" class designed to cast doubt on evolution, I refused to capitulate. I wrote a paper dismantling a fringe theory about "acceleron particles" rather than support the assigned Creationist narrative—and took a failing grade to keep my intellectual integrity.

The Mission
Advocacy & Outreach

Today, my work in outreach is driven by that history. I am dedicated to showing students that science is not a list of beliefs to accept or reject, but a tool to explore the unknown. I fight to protect the "blank page" of discovery for the next generation.
