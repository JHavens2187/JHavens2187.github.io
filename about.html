Who is Joseph Havens?

Astrophysicist by training. Systems Architect by design.

The Scientist

I view the universe as a dataset—messy, chaotic, and governed by impersonal laws—but one that we can understand through rigorous inquiry.

My work focuses on Active Galactic Nuclei (AGN) and the early universe. Using data from the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST), I analyze the spectral fingerprints of cosmic dust to reveal the hidden physics of supermassive black holes. For me, science isn't just a career; it's the fulfillment of a lifelong fight for the right to ask questions.

The Systems Architect

I love building logic engines that create order out of chaos. Whether it's managing real-time tournament brackets or predicting NFL outcomes, I focus on the architecture of the solution.

Recently, I developed a machine learning pipeline that ingests 20 years of NFL statistics, removes external biases, and utilizes ensemble modeling to identify edge cases against Vegas odds. I enjoy the high-level design of software—the flow of logic and state—just as much as the code itself.

The Lens

"Science does not exist in a vacuum. It is done by people, and people are political. My identity as a bi man and my politics as a leftist are not separate from my work—they are the lens through which I see the scientific community."

Visibility as Defiance

Growing up, I watched my school actively remove peers for their queer identities. That erasure taught me that presence is political. By being visibly queer in STEM, I aim to be the evidence I never saw: proof that you can be authentic and scientific at the same time.

Science for the Many

I am an unapologetic leftist because I believe intellectual progress requires material security. We cannot expect the next Einstein to rise from poverty if they are starving. My advocacy for Class Solidarity is directly tied to my belief that science belongs to everyone.

The Origin Story

"These experiences are what forged the person I am today. I am not a scientist despite my upbringing, but because of it. Having lived in a world of prescribed answers, I can never take for granted the profound joy of a genuine question."

The Spark
A Brief History of Time

Reading Hawking in 8th grade ignited a curiosity that couldn't be satisfied by the answers I was being given. It was the first time I realized the universe was larger, older, and more complex than I had been told.

The Crucible
Defending the Data

My high school years were a daily intellectual battle. When authority figures told me that accepting the Big Bang would "send me to hell," it forced me to choose between comfortable conformity and the difficult truth. I chose the truth.

The Mission
The "Blank Page"

Today, my work is driven by a desire to protect the "blank page" of discovery for others. My goal is to dismantle the barriers that tried to stop me, so the next generation can just focus on the stars.
