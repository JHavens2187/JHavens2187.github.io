Who is Joseph Havens?

Astrophysicist by training. Maker by nature.

The Scientist

My academic focus is on Active Galactic Nuclei (AGN) and the early universe. I use data from the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) to peer through cosmic dust and understand how supermassive black holes influence their host galaxies.

I love the rigour of spectral analysis and the challenge of building physical models from data. Currently, I am researching dust attenuation laws using the GLEAM fitting tool.

The Maker

When I'm not analyzing spectra, I'm building things. I have a passion for instrumentation and hardware.

Whether it's designing a high-precision digital multimeter from scratch (Arduino/C++) or 3D printing mounts for a telescope, I believe that understanding the tool is just as important as understanding the science.
