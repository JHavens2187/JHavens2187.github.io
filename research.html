AGN Dust Attenuation & Spectral Analysis with JWST

The Challenge: Seeing Through Cosmic Dust

To understand the first galaxies and their supermassive black holes (AGN), we must first understand the dust that obscures them. A promising method is to compare hydrogen line ratios (e.g., $H\alpha$, $H\beta$, $Pa\beta$).

Ground-based attempts suffer from severe biases like "slit losses" (Prescott et al. 2022). A single, powerful instrument like JWST is required to capture all lines at once.

Using JWST & GLEAM to Solve the Puzzle

My research utilizes new, high-quality data from the JADES and SMACS JWST surveys. I am testing the GLEAM fitting tool to robustly fit key emission lines and measure their flux ratios.

This allows us to constrain the dust attenuation law (both $n$ and $\tau_V$) free from the biases of ground-based data.

Next Steps: Building a Complete Model

We are incorporating Star Formation Rates (SFRs) as an independent constraint. By combining our dust measurements with independent SFRs, we can break model degeneracies.

The goal is to build a complete physical model connecting AGN activity, star formation, and dust in early-universe galaxies.
