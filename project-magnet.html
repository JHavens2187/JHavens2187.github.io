Magnetic Dipole Dynamics

Lagrangian formulation of a symmetric magnetic top in external \(\vec{B}\) and \(\vec{g}\) fields.

TECH STACK

Abstract

This paper derives the equations of motion for a rigid, symmetric body with a magnetic dipole moment \(\vec{\mu}\) subject to both uniform magnetic ($\vec{B}$) and gravitational ($\vec{g}$) fields.

Using the Euler angle formalism ($\phi, \theta, \psi$), we investigated the conditions under which the precession direction reverses when the external magnetic field is inverted, even while the spin of the body remains constant.

The Lagrangian (Eq. 7)
$$ \mathcal{L} = \frac{1}{2}I_{1}(\dot{\phi}^{2}+\dot{\theta}^{2}\sin^{2}\theta) + \frac{1}{2}I_{3}(\dot{\psi}+\dot{\phi}\cos \theta)^{2} - (-\mu B + Mgl)\cos \theta $$

where \(T\) (kinetic energy) utilizes symmetric moments of inertia \(I_1=I_2\), and \(V\) (potential energy) accounts for both magnetic alignment and gravitational height.

Field Reversal & Precession

We derived the approximate precession rate for a "fast top":

$$ \Omega_p \approx \frac{Mgl - \mu B}{p_{\psi}} $$

Crucially, if the magnetic field is reversed ($\vec{B} \to -\vec{B}$), the effective torque changes sign if $|\mu B| \gg |Mgl|$. This theoretically predicts the reversal of precession direction (red arrows) observed in the TeachSpin experiments.
