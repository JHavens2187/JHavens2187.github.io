Digital Multimeter Design

A multi-function measurement system built on the Arduino microcontroller.

TECH STACK
C++ Arduino Circuit Design Error Analysis

Objectives & Hardware

This project implemented a portable digital multimeter capable of measuring voltage, current, capacitance, resistance, and continuity. The system uses an Arduino Uno, utilizing transistors to switch between measurement modes.

  • Voltage: Voltage divider circuit feeding the 10-bit ADC ($0 \text{ V} - 5 \text{ V}$).
  • Current: Shunt resistor ($10 \text{ } \Omega$) in series with the load.
  • Capacitance: RC time constant method ($t = RC$), measuring time to reach $63\%$ of supply voltage.

Error Analysis

The system was rigorously tested against commercial reference units (Fluke 179 and Tenma LCR45).

  • Voltage Accuracy: $\approx 1\%$ error margin (comparable to Fluke 179 at $0.9\%$).
  • Current Accuracy: $\approx 5\%$ error margin due to shunt resistor tolerance and ADC quantization.
  • Capacitance: Large discrepancy ($\approx 30\%$) observed, likely due to capacitor tolerance issues.
