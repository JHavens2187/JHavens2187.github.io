PYAXIOMA-JH Methodology

Non-contact angular displacement measurement using computer vision and optical flow.

TECH STACK
Python OpenCV Hough Transforms Morphological Filters

The Problem

Measuring the angular displacement of a magnetic torque oscillator typically requires physical sensors (rotary encoders) that introduce friction and damping, altering the system's natural physics.

The Solution: I developed PYAXIOMA-JH, a computer vision pipeline that tracks colored markers via a standard 1080p webcam. This allows for the capture of torque-induced oscillations and precession without physical contact.

The Pipeline

  • 1. Detection: Used Hough Circle Transform and HSV color masking to locate the cue ball and isolate the marker.
  • 2. Motion Masking: Implemented frame differencing with a binary threshold ($1.75$) to create a motion mask, removing static background noise.
  • 3. Filtering: Applied a $5 \times 5$ morphological closing operation to bridge gaps in the motion contours.
  • 4. Tracking: Calculated the centroid $(c_{x, \text{black}}, c_{y, \text{black}})$ of the largest motion contour to determine the marker's precise location.
angle_computation.py
$$ \theta = \arccos \left( \frac{\vec{A} \cdot \vec{B}}{|\vec{A}| |\vec{B}|} \right) $$

Where vector $\vec{A}$ connects the cue ball center to the marker, and $\vec{B}$ is the horizontal reference vector. Quadrant-specific corrections were applied to map the result to a full $[-90^{\circ}, 270^{\circ}]$ range.

Result Analysis

The system successfully tracked the damped harmonic motion of the oscillator. The sinusoidal trend superimposed on the exponential decay (seen in the graph) reveals the precessional motion of the axis.

The blue dots represent the calculated Confidence Score, a metric I developed based on angular velocity and jerk to filter out low-quality frames.
