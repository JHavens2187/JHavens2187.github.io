Stellar Cluster Ages

Isochrone fitting of 12 star clusters using GAIA DR3 photometry data.

TECH STACK
Python Pandas GAIA Mission Isochrones

Methodology

We analyzed 6 Open Clusters and 6 Globular Clusters using data from the ESA's GAIA mission. By plotting the stars on a Color-Magnitude Diagram (HR Diagram), we fit theoretical "Isochrones" (lines of equal age) to estimate the cluster's formation time.

Stars were de-reddened to account for extinction, and filters were applied based on parallax and proper motion.

Results

The analysis confirmed a distinct age gap between the two cluster types:

  • Open Clusters (e.g., M11, M45): Ages ranged from $8.2$ to $8.9 \text{ Gyr}$.
  • Globular Clusters (e.g., M2, M13): Significantly older, ranging from $8.5$ to $9.8 \text{ Gyr}$.
  • Galactic Correlation: A slight correlation was found between age and galactic latitude (distance from the galactic plane), suggesting older clusters reside in the outer halo.
